Your Sketch license
explained

Here’s what you need to know about your Sketch license, based on
the questions we get asked the most.

Where can I find the End User License Agreement (EULA)?

You can read the Sketch End User License Agreement here, which
is a legal contract between a software application author or publisher
and the user of that application.

How long does my Sketch license last?

Your license is valid for a year from the date you purchase it. For
example, if you purchase your license on 26th June, 2019, you’ll get
regular updates to Sketch up to and including 25th June, 2020. To
receive updates after that date, you’ll need to renew your license.

An active Sketch license also enables you to upload your documents to
our Sketch Cloud service.

What happens if I don’t renew my license?

If you don’t want to renew your license after it expires, you can continue
to use the version of Sketch that you have, for as long as you want, but
you won’t be able to receive any further updates. You will no longer be
able to upload new documents to Sketch Cloud but you can still sign in
to Sketch Cloud to view, comment on, and download documents. Any
documents you previously uploaded will stay on Cloud until you choose
to remove them. If you decide you want to renew at a later date, that’s
fine — you’re welcome back at any time.

Do I need to renew my license to receive fixes?

We split up Sketch releases into regular updates and bug-fix updates.
You’ll always be entitled to a bug-fix update for your last version, even
after your license has expired.

Any additional questions, please check out our Licensing and
Sales support section or feel free to contact us.
